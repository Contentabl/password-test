  • the Selective Carbohydrate Diet
  • Paleo 
  • Primal/Weston A. Price's Nourishing Traditions
  • Ketogenic/ High-Fat, Low-Carbohydrate
  • Hypoallergenic
  • Low/No-FODMAPs
  • Autoimmune Paleo/Wahls Protocol

Nutritional Consulting

So, you've just been diagnosed with a health condition...

...and you're totally overwhelmed. All the things you could eat before are off-limits. What do you do? You call us. Get a chef's perspective on how to navigate the grocery store, the kitchen, the farmer's market. We talk, we look through your kitchen. We shop for food. I leave you empowered, with a fridge full of food that's safe for you to eat. And you'll even get a week of unlimited e-mail support after your consultation. You can even combine your consultation with cooking lessons, so that you feel ready to take on your new lifestyle head-on where it counts: the kitchen.

Special Occasions and Special Requests

We are able to take on a very limited number of special requests, but if you need an autoimmune-friendly holiday meal, paleo truffles for a significant other's birthday, or perhaps an allergy-free engagement dinner for just you and your honey... we might just be able to help you out. Don't hesitate; no request goes unconsidered!

Who We Are

From our chef, Ari Botsford:

If you had told me two years ago that I was making myself sick with what I was eating, I would laugh. I'd stuff my face with pizza, and I'd laugh at you. Now? Now, it's a much different story. I'm helping people all over New York feel amazing by changing what they eat. But it's not just my undying love of agriculture, science, and gourmet pursuits that forms my personal philosophy. I've been managing my autoimmune issues with food for many years now, and I believe you can too.


My story starts some 1, 193 miles away. I grew up on a farm in The Ozarks. My father won many awards for his work in rotational grazing and high-quality, organically-raised, pastured meats. Serving as director of both our local chapters of the Weston A. Price Foundation, and The League of Women Voters, I was brought up with strong values and respect for agriculture, nutrition, and the activism necessary to sustain both. With 250 acres to roam on, I spent much time learning how to care for the livestock we sold to feed families in southwest Missouri. Combined with my mother's adventurous spirit and dedication to nutritive dishes in the kitchen, I grew up eating fresh, epicurean foods straight from our ranch to our dinner table. From gathering eggs to bottle-feeding calves, riding horses to herding sheep, our grass-fed farm laid the roots for my love of seasonal, gourmet food.

I left Missouri in 2007, and headed east to Brooklyn. I ended up here by accident; my intent was to pursue filmmaking. Turns out, my body had other plans for me. At age nineteen, I experienced my first anaphylaxis. After eating my typical nut-and-fruit bar on the train en route to class, my throat swelled shut. It was after that first experience that I was told I had a tree nut allergy. And it continued. Serious allergies and intolerances to eggs, peanuts, and soy appeared. I started cooking, a lot. I couldn't just grab anything and go, I had to know what my food was made of. So, off to school I went-- except that a shrimp lunch put me in an ambulance, the day before I started my culinary arts track at The Institute of Culinary Education.

By the time I graduated, I was unable to consume all of the top 8 allergens. On top of this, I was suffering from chronic gastritis, gastric ulcers, GERD and horrible fatigue. My anxiety issues were a mess. My joints heaved and creaked, my anxiety left me panicking and dissociated most days. Dozens of doctors had no idea what my problem was-- autoimmune issue, sure--but what? Without a diagnosis, and without a clue, I was, in short, falling apart. After a few years of bad relationships, unnecessary stress, and debilitating sickness, I finally accepted that my mind, body, and food were connected. I had to be nicer to myself---and that meant nourishing myself with the right ingredients and activities.

These days, I follow a Paleo diet with strong influences from the nutrient-dense Wahls Protocol and Nourishing Traditions. I walk and run more than ten miles per day. Sleep gets prioritized above almost anything else. I changed the entire way I live, and I've never felt better. The farmer's market is my second home, after my butcher shops, of course. The food I grow allows me a deep connection to the food I make. You'll often find vegetables from my organic, heirloom garden on the end of your fork, especially during the growing season. My life as a former farm girl makes a strong presence in my cooking, as does my empathy for whatever you may be going through. I understand how terrified you are. And I understand how eating the right food can change your life. On top of all that-- we know what it's like to be too busy.

We get it.

This is why we do what we do. And that's why we make nutrient-dense, farm-to-table, customized-to-need fare for New Yorkers across the city.
